Listings

The Stanley Hotel
Estes Park, Colorado

An iconic piece of Colorado's storied history, the Stanley Hotel sits grandly in the Rocky Mountains, offering panoramic views. Its claim to supernatural fame was cemented when Stephen King's stay here inspired him to write "The Shining." Over the years, guests have reported ghostly figures, laughter, and piano music echoing in its halls, especially on the famed fourth floor.

The Queen Mary
Long Beach, California

Once a magnificent ocean liner setting records for speed, luxury, and elegance, The Queen Mary is now a floating hotel, rich in history and tales of spectral sightings. Among the numerous ghosts purported to haunt the ship, the most well-known stories involve apparitions in the engine room, where a sailor is believed to have died, and the sounds of children playing by the pool area.

Lizzie Borden Bed & Breakfast
Fall River, Massachusetts

This unassuming home is the site of one of America's most infamous unsolved mysteries. In 1892, Lizzie Borden was accused and later acquitted of brutally murdering her father and stepmother with an axe. Today, guests can stay in the meticulously restored bed & breakfast, where many have reported eerie phenomena, especially in the rooms where the murders took place.

Myrtles Plantation
St. Francisville, Louisiana

Touted as one of America's most haunted homes, Myrtles Plantation is a beautiful antebellum home surrounded by ancient oaks draped in Spanish moss. Legends of the plantation suggest up to 10 murders within its walls, although historical records only confirm one. Guests and staff tell tales of apparitions, especially of a former slave named Chloe, who is said to wander the grounds, recognizable by her turban.
